## Source Position

### What Chapter 6 Contributes

Chapter 6 revises the classical Jungian "every dream is compensatory" position and replaces it with a more nuanced taxonomy. It introduces the compensation/complementation distinction, the object/subject level triage, and the projection model. It provides operational rules for choosing the most productive interpretation level. It also contains the most extensive clinical firewall section in the source so far: the fragile/undeveloped ego section, which is entirely CLINICAL_ONLY.

**What Chapter 6 gives Iris:**
- The compensation/complementation distinction as interpretive orientation
- The rule that the most productive level is the one "most at variance" with the dreamer's current position
- Day residue handling: usually subject level; except when day event was forgotten or minimized
- Overdramatization caution: compensatory exaggeration ≠ literal finality
- Dream considered inadequate until it reveals a blind spot or challenges a fixated position
- Projection as "intense affect + overreaction" signal in waking life

**What stays clinical:**
- All of the undeveloped/fragmented ego section (Ch. 6 pp.63-66)
- Therapist using dream without sharing it with patient
- Transference/countertransference dynamics in compensation

### What Chapter 7 Contributes

Chapter 7 provides the structural template for organizing dream content into a readable drama. It is highly operational: exposition = problem statement; peripeteia = development; crisis = culmination; lysis/catastrophe = outcome. It explicitly states that lysis/catastrophe is prospective possibility, not fixed fate. It provides the skeleton summary tool for chaotic long dreams and explicitly states that "solving a problem only in dream is not sufficient."

**What Chapter 7 gives Iris:**
- Drama structure as an analytical grid for any dream
- Exposition = "Re:" — always unpack the opening scene
- Sequence as possible causal chain
- Protagonist/antagonist structure as polarity map
- Lysis/catastrophe as possibility, not prophecy
- Waking responsibility is required — dream insight alone is not enough
- Chaotic long dreams: skeleton summary first
- Narrative tone and vocabulary as data
- NOT happening in dream = as important as what IS happening

**What stays clinical:**
- Diagnostic value of structural matters (noted but firewalled)
- Object-level prophecy hints (judgment call reserved for operator, not Iris)

### How Block 3 Differs from Blocks 1-2

Block 1 established the epistemological frame and reading contract. Block 2 established the step-by-step procedure (association → explanation → amplification → affect charge). Block 3 adds two interpretive tools that sit above the procedural layer:
1. **Ch. 6**: Interpretive orientation — which level to work, what the dream is doing relative to the dreamer's stance
2. **Ch. 7**: Structural analysis — reading the dream as a whole dramatic arc, not just image by image

Block 3 is less about individual image handling and more about the WHOLE DREAM as a unit.

### Operationally Valuable for Iris

- Compensation/complementation as orientation tools when multiple readings are possible
- Object/subject triage rule (most complementary level = most at variance with conscious stance)
- Day residue rule (subject level by default, with exception)
- Overdramatization caution (catastrophic imagery ≠ literal fate)
- Exposition-as-"Re:" approach (opening scene as problem statement)
- Drama skeleton summary for chaotic dreams
- Lysis/catastrophe as prospective possibility framing
- Waking responsibility without command (dream solved ≠ done; still needs waking action)

### Clinically Risky If Imported Directly

- Fragile/undeveloped ego handling (entirely clinical)
- Using dream as data for the therapist, not the dreamer
- Diagnostic value of structural matters (clinical authority)
- Object-level prophecy judgment (Iris cannot assess; offer as possibility only)

## Findings From Text

### Finding B3-001 — Not every dream is simply compensatory: multiple types
- **evidence_status:** FOUND_IN_TEXT
- **source:** Ch. 6, p.56 (stream 150)
- **what text says:** Jung spoke of dreams that compensate; dreams at variance with the conscious position; dreams that challenge and aim at changing orientation; dreams more or less unrelated (prospective); parallel dreams that "coincide with or support the conscious attitude." All are ways of "presenting the situation as it is."
- **operational relevance:** Iris does not mechanically assume every dream is opposing the dreamer's conscious stance. Dream type must be read from the material: compensatory opposition, complementary addition, supportive parallel, or prospective possibility.
- **clinical component:** The chapter opens with the caveat about severely wounded/regressed clients who "cannot find a consistent ego position" — CLINICAL_ONLY.
- **risk/firewall:** NEEDS_FIREWALL: Iris must not force every dream into a compensatory frame.
- **confidence:** HIGH

### Finding B3-002 — Compensation vs complementation: precise distinction
- **evidence_status:** FOUND_IN_TEXT
- **source:** Ch. 6, p.57 (stream 152)
- **what text says:** **Compensation** = brings forth "often in exaggerated ways, the polar opposites of our conscious views" (too optimistic view → presented as quite dismal). **Complementation** = "adds any missing pieces, not necessarily the polar opposite ones" ("look also at this and this and also that"). Both serve to correct blind spots and widen awareness. Compensation challenges; complementation adds.
- **operational relevance:** Iris distinguishes between these when orienting to a dream. A dream that shows the absolute opposite of the dreamer's view = compensation. A dream that adds elements the dreamer didn't consider = complementation. A dream that simply supports an uncertain conscious position = parallel.
- **clinical component:** None.
- **risk/firewall:** None.
- **confidence:** HIGH

### Finding B3-003 — Dream as completing what consciousness leaves incomplete
- **evidence_status:** FOUND_IN_TEXT

- **operational relevance:** The dream fills in what consciousness is currently missing. Iris approaches each dream with the question: "What might be missing from this person's current picture of themselves or their situation that the dream is adding?"

### Finding B3-005 — Object vs subject level triage: choose by complementary function
- **evidence_status:** FOUND_IN_TEXT
- **source:** Ch. 6, p.59 (stream 156)
- **what text says:** "Bearing in mind the likely complementary function, we might be helped at times in deciding whether a dream is to be looked upon as pertaining to subject or object level. - **operational relevance:** CRITICAL triage rule for Iris. When ambiguous: ask "on which level would this dream provide something new, something at variance with the dreamer's current view?" That is the more likely correct level.

### Finding B3-006 — Object level first if dream adds new warning; subject level if repeated/known

### Finding B3-007 — Day residue repetition = subject level; exception is forgotten/minimized detail

### Finding B3-008 — Projection: intense affect + overreaction as signal
- **evidence_status:** FOUND_IN_TEXT
- **source:** Ch. 6, p.59-60 (streams 156-158)
- **what text says:** Projection = "expression of an as yet unrealized unconscious tendency, seen in the image of an external object or person that most adequately represents it to the dreamer." - **operational relevance:** When operator shows or describes intense affective overreaction to a dream figure, Iris may note (gently) the possibility that the figure carries a projected quality. Not as a certainty — as a question: "What is it about [person] that elicits that reaction in you?"

- **risk/firewall:** NEEDS_FIREWALL: Iris does not tell operator "you are projecting." Iris explores: "What does [person] bring up in you?"

### Finding B3-011 — Overdramatization: compensatory exaggeration ≠ literal finality
- **evidence_status:** FOUND_IN_TEXT
- **source:** Ch. 6, p.61 (stream 160)
- **what text says:** Compensating dreams "often appear to exaggerate and overdramatize." Example: repressing a quality → dream shows dreamer murdering the person carrying the projection of that quality. Compensation "roughly proportional to the dreamer's own deviation from the truth." Color analogy: dreamer sees green as blue → dream shows yellow (blue + yellow = green). "We may become unduly alarmed by overdramatizations, dreams depicting catastrophes, murder, mayhem... Such dreams, usually, are trying to 'rub it in.' When we are not aware of this compensatory tendency, we may wrongly assume a finality of dramatic outcomes where there is no more than a temporary compensation."
- **operational relevance:** When dream content is extreme (murder, catastrophe, violent imagery), Iris considers whether compensation is at play — the dream exaggerating to match the dreamer's degree of blindness. Extreme imagery ≠ literal prediction or command. Iris may name: "This dream seems to be pushing hard on something. The intensity might be proportional to how much this particular aspect has been overlooked."
- **clinical component:** None.
- **risk/firewall:** Reinforces B1-020/RISK-WP-001: conditional outcome. Adds: exaggeration is a known mechanism, not a sign of literal severity.
- **confidence:** HIGH
