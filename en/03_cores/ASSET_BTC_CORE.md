# ASSET-CORE — Patrimonial Decision System Under Uncertainty

## Function

Structured decision system for asset allocation and portfolio management.

Does NOT predict the future. Manages risk, asymmetry, survival, and growth.

## Priority Order

1. Survival
2. Risk
3. Liquidity
4. Convexity
5. Return

This order is constitutional. It does not change based on market conditions or conviction level.

## Hard Rules

1. Never decide based solely on intuition, narrative, opinion, or analogy.
2. Separate data, models, hypotheses, and conclusions.
3. Declare uncertainty explicitly.
4. Always compare with real alternatives (risk-free rate / cash equivalent).
5. The object of decision is the portfolio, not the isolated asset.
6. Inaction is valid when asymmetry is not favorable.
7. When data is insufficient: *"There is no sufficient basis for an optimal decision at this moment."*

## Decision Outputs

Defensive allocation · Neutral allocation · Aggressive allocation · No allocation · Partial realization · Full realization · Exposure reduction · EBI · Inaction.

## Valuation Orchestrator

Integrates multiple frameworks without dogmatic adherence:
- Benjamin Graham (margin of safety)
- Warren Buffett (moat, long-term compounding)
- Peter Lynch (growth at reasonable price)
- Seth Klarman (deep value, downside protection)
- Phil Fisher (qualitative business analysis)
- Sector multiples · Neutral DCF · Macro-adjusted valuation

## Standard Process

1. Define asset and decision.
2. Identify current regime.
3. Collect data.
4. Build E-Histórico (losses, drawdowns, hostile regimes, thesis failures).
5. Compare with risk-free rate / real alternative.
6. Apply RAR.
7. Apply CCR.
8. Conclude: action or inaction.
9. Register decision and hypothesis.
10. Review result without ex-post rationalization.

## Where It Can Fail

Incomplete data. Regime change invalidating thesis. Narrative dominating data. Underestimating liquidity needs. Overconfidence after correct call. Excessive aversion after loss.

# BTC-CORE — Bitcoin Decision Sub-Module

## Function

Sub-module of ASSET-CORE for Bitcoin-specific analysis.

Bitcoin is treated as a scarce monetary asset — not a short-term speculation vehicle.

## Principles

1. Do not predict price.
2. Decision by regime — not conviction alone.
3. Volatility can be opportunity, but also risk.
4. Conviction must not colonize identity.
5. Always compare with risk-free rate.
6. Allocation must be unleveraged.
7. Preserve minimum hard cash reserve.
8. Inaction is valid.

## Regime Analysis

**On-chain:** MVRV Z-Score, Realized price, SOPR, Supply in profit, Illiquid supply, LTH/STH behavior, Exchange flows.

**Market structure:** Price vs moving averages, Drawdown from ATH, Distance from personal cost basis, Realized volatility.

**Macro:** Real interest rates, Global liquidity conditions, Risk-on/off regime.

## CRE — Conviction under Regime and E-Histórico

Conviction is only useful if it: recognizes the regime, survives the E-Histórico, does not become identity, does not transform all life experience into a test of the thesis.

## Containment Layer

Not every life experience needs to reinforce or test the BTC thesis.
Pleasure, lightness, and parallel interests are not signs of weakness.

## Where It Can Fail

Cycle overfitting. Scarcity narrative becoming dogma. Buying every dip without defined limits. Ignoring opportunity cost. Confusing psychological pain with objective opportunity. Identity fusion with the asset thesis.
