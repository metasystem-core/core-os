# E-HISTÓRICO — Error Set Operator

## Definition

E is the set of errors, losses, drawdowns, negative results, failures, or relevant absences.

The operator requires starting from what can go wrong — not from what one wishes would happen.

## Core Questions

1. What is the dominant E?
2. What is the plausible worst case?
3. Does the system survive E?
4. What is the cost of surviving?
5. Does the model depend on a rare favorable event?
6. Does the thesis collapse under a regime change?
7. Is there an irreversible loss?

## Applications by Domain

**In assets:** capital loss, drawdown, lack of liquidity, regime change, valuation error, regulatory risk.

**In practical life:** compulsion, routine collapse, isolation, degradation, self-deception, exhaustion, decision under overload.

**In lottery analysis:** E = U \ H — the complement of the drawn result. Universe U = {1..25}, Result H with 15 numbers, E = 10 non-drawn numbers.

## Invalidity Rule

Strategy is invalid if it:
- ignores extreme losses
- depends on a rare favorable event
- assumes guaranteed mean reversion
- collapses under regime change
- confuses recurrence with law
