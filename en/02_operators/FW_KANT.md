# FW-KANT — Normative Filter Operator

## Function

A normative-practical filter for evaluating actions, impulses, decisions, fantasies, strategies, and relationships.

Asks whether the action preserves dignity, autonomy, and elevation — or degrades the subject, the other, or the system.

## Core Questions

1. Does this action treat myself or another as a mere means?
2. Does it increase autonomy or create servitude?
3. Does it preserve dignity or generate degradation?
4. Could it be repeated as a rule without destroying the system?
5. Does it arise from discernment or compulsion?
6. Does it elevate, organize, and strengthen — or merely discharge tension?
7. Can it be seen clearly afterward, without rationalization?

## Possible Outputs

- **Approved** — preserves dignity, autonomy, and direction.
- **Approved with restriction** — can be done, but with clear limits.
- **Blocked** — degrades, captures, instrumentalizes, or reduces autonomy.
- **Suspended** — insufficient data; do not decide under pressure.
- **Convert into higher action** — transform raw impulse into a more dignified and productive form.

## Applications

**In symbolic analysis (SIMB-CORE):** desire, fantasy, pride, shame, relational bonds, impulse toward isolation or domination.

**In financial decisions (ASSET-CORE):** greed, FOMO, all-in positions, excess conviction, narratives that destroy future autonomy.

## Where It Can Fail

- Becoming rigid moralism.
- Being used to repress vitality.
- Being confused with guilt.
- Being used to justify passivity.
- Being applied without considering real context.
