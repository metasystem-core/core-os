## Structural Assessment

### What kind of book is this?

**Practical clinical handbook for Jungian dream-work**, written by two practicing analysts for analysts and therapists. NOT primarily theoretical — it is procedurally organized, moving from foundational concepts through method and finally to clinical challenges.

Orientation: how to work with dreams in therapeutic settings. The authors assume a clinical frame: the reader is a therapist; the dreamer is a patient/analysand; the interaction is analytic. The book's method is Jungian but more accessible and systematic than Jung's collected essays.

Evidence status: FOUND_IN_TEXT — "The clinical understanding of dreams requires both art and skill." (Ch. 2)

### Is it theory, clinical manual, practical primer, symbolic method, or mixed?

**Mixed: practical primer + clinical manual.** Chapters 1-9 function as a practical primer (concept + method + examples). Chapters 10-12 are explicitly clinical (prognosis, diagnosis, transference). The theory is embedded within practical examples, not treated in isolation.

### What does it add after Jung Dreams (SRC-SIMB-011)?
